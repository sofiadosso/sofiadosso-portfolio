Sofia Dosso

Living o'clock

Interior design

2022

The project is located inside the garden of Casa Chiaravalle, a green area near Milan, where assistance is offered to people suffering from Alzheimer’s. The concept stems from the desire to prevent a problem that often emerges in people affected by the disease, i.e. the loss of sleep-wake rhythm with the consequent appearance of the Sundowning syndrome. The goal of the project is to help the elderly to resume this daily rhythm through the space of the house.

The house includes the use of light and neutral colors. Each room is characterized by the presence of a boiserie painted on the wall in a different color: in this way, it is possible to further identify the various spaces. In addition, some rooms, such as the living room and the bedroom, are distinguished by the presence of decorative graphics on the walls. Most of the furnishings are characterized by rounded shapes and they help to “delimit” the different areas, without closing them completely.

The residence is entirely designed following the natural cycle of the sun and the rooms are arranged in order of use. Upon entering, you will come across the living area, then the kitchen, bathroom and finally the sleeping area. They are connected through the side corridor equipped with large windows. The fundamental element of the project is light: in fact, there is an automatic lighting system that provides for the gradual switching on of lights that follow the solar cycle.
